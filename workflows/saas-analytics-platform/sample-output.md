## A) Core SaaS Metrics Framework

- **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue):** MRR = Sum of all active subscription values normalized to one month. Tracks the predictable revenue heartbeat of the business; movement in MRR is the primary signal of growth or decline.
- **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue):** ARR = MRR × 12. Converts monthly momentum into an annualized view used for valuation, fundraising benchmarks, and long-range planning.
- **Churn Rate:** Churn Rate = (Customers Lost in Period ÷ Customers at Start of Period) × 100. Measures retention failure; even a 2–3% monthly churn compounds into catastrophic revenue loss over 12 months.
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Spend ÷ New Customers Acquired. Quantifies the cost efficiency of growth; rising CAC without rising LTV signals a deteriorating go-to-market engine.
- **LTV (Customer Lifetime Value):** LTV = ARPU ÷ Monthly Churn Rate. Represents the total revenue a customer generates before churning; the foundational input for unit economics viability.
- **LTV:CAC Ratio:** LTV:CAC = LTV ÷ CAC. The definitive unit economics health check; a ratio below 3:1 indicates the business is acquiring customers at unsustainable cost relative to their value.
- **NRR (Net Revenue Retention):** NRR = (Starting MRR + Expansion − Contraction − Churn) ÷ Starting MRR × 100. Measures whether the existing customer base grows revenue on its own; NRR above 100% means the business can grow without acquiring a single new customer.
- **Activation Rate:** Activation Rate = (Users Reaching Activation Milestone ÷ Total Signups) × 100. Tracks the percentage of new users who reach the product's core value moment; low activation is the earliest predictor of future churn.
- **Expansion Revenue:** Expansion MRR = MRR added from upsells, cross-sells, and seat additions from existing customers. Directly improves NRR and reduces dependence on new customer acquisition to hit growth targets.
- **CAC Payback Period:** Payback Period = CAC ÷ (ARPU × Gross Margin %). Measures how many months it takes to recover acquisition cost; payback periods exceeding 18 months create dangerous cash flow exposure in downturns.

## B) Revenue Projection Model

The analytics engine projects forward MRR by treating revenue as a dynamic flow with four discrete inputs: new customer acquisition, churn-driven contraction, pricing-tier-weighted ARPU, and expansion revenue from the existing base. Each projection period ingests the current customer count segmented by pricing tier, applies the observed or forecasted acquisition rate, subtracts expected churn using a rolling cohort-adjusted churn rate, and adds expansion revenue derived from historical upsell velocity. This approach produces a range-bound forecast — base case, optimistic, and stress scenario — rather than a single-line projection, giving operators a probabilistic view of where MRR lands under different retention and acquisition assumptions.

Formula name: **Projected MRR(t)**  
Expression:  
Projected MRR(t) = Current MRR + (New Customers × ARPU) − (Churned Customers × ARPU) + Expansion Revenue

The model layers in a compounding growth adjustment for multi-period forecasts, recognizing that each period's ending MRR becomes the next period's starting base. Gross margin is applied to convert MRR projections into contribution margin forecasts, enabling operators to see not just revenue growth but cash-generative growth. Sensitivity tables allow operators to stress-test the model by adjusting churn rate, ARPU, or acquisition rate independently.

Formula name: **LTV-Adjusted Revenue Run Rate**  
Expression:  
Revenue Run Rate = ARR × (NRR ÷ 100) × (1 − Projected Annual Churn Rate)

This adjusted run rate strips out revenue that is statistically unlikely to persist, giving operators a conservative but defensible forward revenue figure for board reporting, hiring plans, and infrastructure spend commitments.

## C) Early Warning Risk Signals

- **Churn Spike Detection:** Triggered when monthly churn rate increases more than 1.5× its 90-day rolling average. Signals a product, pricing, or competitive disruption event requiring immediate cohort-level investigation before the damage compounds into MRR collapse.
- **Declining Activation Rate:** Triggered when the 30-day activation rate drops more than 15% week-over-week. Activation failure is a leading indicator of future churn; customers who never reach the value moment cancel within 60–90 days at disproportionately high rates.
- **CAC Payback Deterioration:** Triggered when payback period extends beyond a configurable threshold (default: 18 months) for two consecutive months. Indicates that sales and marketing efficiency is degrading, often preceding a cash runway crisis if uncorrected.
- **Revenue Concentration Risk:** Triggered when any single customer or customer segment exceeds 15% of total MRR. Flags dangerous dependency; loss of one concentrated account can instantly breach financial covenants or force emergency cost cuts.
- **NRR Compression:** Triggered when NRR falls below 100% for two consecutive months. Means the existing customer base is shrinking in revenue terms, forcing the business to run faster on new acquisition just to stay flat — a structurally unsustainable position.
- **Expansion Revenue Stall:** Triggered when expansion MRR growth rate drops below 5% month-over-month after a period of consistent growth. Signals saturation of upsell motion or product ceiling effects, directly threatening the NRR trajectory and long-term LTV assumptions.

```python
# SaaS Analytics Core Metrics Calculator
# Demonstrates MRR, Churn Rate, and LTV from raw customer data

# Sample customer dataset
customers = [
    {"customer_id": "C001", "mrr": 299,  "status": "active",   "months_active": 14},
    {"customer_id": "C002", "mrr": 99,   "status": "active",   "months_active": 6},
    {"customer_id": "C003", "mrr": 499,  "status": "churned",  "months_active": 3},
    {"customer_id": "C004", "mrr": 199,  "status": "active",   "months_active": 22},
    {"customer_id": "C005", "mrr": 99,   "status": "churned",  "months_active": 2},
    {"customer_id": "C006", "mrr": 999,  "status": "active",   "months_active": 18},
    {"customer_id": "C007", "mrr": 299,  "status": "active",   "months_active": 9},
    {"customer_id": "C008", "mrr": 199,  "status": "churned",  "months_active": 5},
    {"customer_id": "C009", "mrr": 499,  "status": "active",   "months_active": 31},
    {"customer_id": "C010", "mrr": 99,   "status": "active",   "months_active": 7},
]

def calculate_mrr(customers):
    return sum(c["mrr"] for c in customers if c["status"] == "active")

def calculate_churn_rate(customers):
    total = len(customers)
    churned = sum(1 for c in customers if c["status"] == "churned")
    return (churned / total * 100) if total > 0 else 0.0

def calculate_ltv(customers):
    if not customers:
        return 0.0
    avg_mrr = sum(c["mrr"] for c in customers) / len(customers)
    avg_months_active = sum(c["months_active"] for c in customers) / len(customers)
    return avg_mrr * avg_months_active

mrr = calculate_mrr(customers)
churn_rate = calculate_churn_rate(customers)
ltv = calculate_ltv(customers)

print("MRR:", mrr)
print("Churn Rate:", churn_rate)
print("LTV:", ltv)
```
