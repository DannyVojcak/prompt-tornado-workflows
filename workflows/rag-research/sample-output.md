## B) Core RAG Architecture Patterns

- **Adaptive RAG** dynamically classifies query complexity and routes requests between direct LLM response, single-step retrieval, and multi-step retrieval chains to minimize cost while preserving accuracy.
- **Self-RAG** trains models to emit special reflection tokens that trigger on-demand retrieval and self-critique of generated passages against retrieved evidence, reducing hallucination at the architectural level.
- **Agentic RAG** embeds a planning and reasoning loop around retrieval, enabling multi-hop queries, dynamic namespace selection, and iterative evidence gathering where single-pass lookup fails.
- **Hybrid search** couples BM25 sparse retrieval with dense vector search and applies cross-encoder reranking to produce a unified relevance-ranked candidate set before generation.
- **Hierarchical and semantic chunking strategies** segment source documents into variable-length, semantically coherent units rather than fixed-size windows, improving embedding quality and retrieval precision for long-form enterprise content.

## D) Enterprise Implementation Challenges

- Hallucination persists even in well-tuned RAG systems when retrieved context is incomplete, contradictory, or silently irrelevant, requiring architectural mitigations such as Self-RAG reflection rather than relying on LLM capability alone.
- Retrieval quality degrades sharply on multi-hop and comparative queries that require synthesizing evidence across multiple documents, exposing the limits of single-pass vector lookup without agentic planning.
- Cost control is non-trivial because accurate query complexity classification is itself a compute task, and misclassification either wastes the savings of adaptive routing or silently degrades output quality.
- Multi-stage retrieval pipelines introduce compounding latency across embedding, search, reranking, and generation steps, requiring parallel execution strategies and aggressive caching to meet enterprise SLA thresholds.
- Data governance in hybrid retrieval architectures is structurally complex because queries span multiple vector stores, keyword indexes, and external APIs, creating access-control gaps that standard RAG frameworks do not address natively.
- No industry-standardized benchmarking framework for enterprise RAG exists as of 2025, leaving organizations to define bespoke evaluation suites that are difficult to compare across vendors or internal iterations.

## E) Strategic Design Recommendations

- **Implement adaptive query routing** as the first architectural decision, classifying requests by complexity before any retrieval occurs to allocate compute proportionally and prevent cost overruns at scale.
- **Instrument every retrieval stage with span-level observability** from day one, capturing embedding latency, reranking scores, and faithfulness signals to enable data-driven pipeline iteration rather than intuition-driven tuning.
- **Layer RAG with domain-specific mid-training** on proprietary corpora so that the embedding model and the generative model share a common semantic space, reducing retrieval mismatch and improving factual grounding on specialized enterprise content.
